Biography

I received my Ph.D. focusing on P4-programmable data planes for offloading network security functionalities. My doctoral research investigated how to implement Deep Packet Inspection (DPI) at line rate, with a particular emphasis on detecting malicious attacks that exploit the Domain Name System (DNS).
Alongside my research, I developed extensive hands-on instructional materials in networking and cybersecurity, covering topics such as BGP, OSPF, Software-Defined Networking (SDN), programmable data planes, and applied cybersecurity.
My current research builds on this foundation and expands into security for Industrial Control Systems (ICS), hybrid network- and host-based defense mechanisms, attacks on generative AI systems, and next-generation high-performance technologies, including Smart Network Interface Cards (SmartNICs), P4, and eBPF.
